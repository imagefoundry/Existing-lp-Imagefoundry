Workflow

Start with a still.
End with a
dynamic still.

Our typical flow is simple. Start with a finished CGI still. Use NanoBanana 2.5 to add lifelike characters — try two or three versions to get the right mix of poses and density. Then bring that image into Runway. Generate a short camera move. Think of a 7 to 10 second glide.

That is a dynamic still.

Quality control · Flexibility

The golden five
or six seconds
that are flawless.

Quality control happens in the edit. AI is convincing until a tiny quirk sneaks in on a frame. On a slow move through a busy scene you might see an odd hand or a face that breaks for a beat.

We trim to the golden five or six seconds that are flawless.

The result is a tight, believable clip with people who feel present in the space. The flexibility is a bonus — from one base image we can create multiple cuts for different markets and audiences with different demographic make ups. We guide the AI with simple prompts: rough headcount, what they are doing, dress code, broad age range. It stays close to the intent and looks authentic.
