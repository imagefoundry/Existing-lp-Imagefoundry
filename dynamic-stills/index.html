What Are Dynamic Stills

Motion from
a single still

"Your CGI stills represent a significant investment. Dynamic Stills unlock a second life for that asset — real motion, zero re-render."

Dynamic stills let us bring movement to a scene that was never modelled for animation. We push, pull, pan, tilt or glide through a space — the AI fills in what the camera uncovers as it moves.

The result is a short, polished clip ready for social, digital advertising or pitch decks. One asset. A fraction of the time. Genuinely cinematic output.

The Process

How it
works

A single still image is fed into AI software designed for motion generation. We provide a prompt, the system generates a short animated clip — usually only a few seconds long.

01
Feed in the still

A single CGI still is loaded into AI motion software. No additional modelling, rigging or scene setup required — the still itself is the starting point.

02
Write the prompt

We provide a prompt that gives broad direction such as the type of camera movement. Simple, direct prompts produce the most consistent results.

03
Generate & edit

The system generates a short clip — the initial few seconds tend to be most useful. Multiple clips edited together create a polished narrative quickly.

Honest Assessment

The Verdict

Pros
+
Fast and cost-effective

Minutes or hours — not days or weeks. A fraction of traditional animation costs.

+
Lightweight and flexible

Ideal for short-form content — social, email, digital ads — where a little movement goes a long way.

Cons
−
Limited control

Broad direction only. Fine adjustments to timing or specific object movement aren't possible.

−
Simplistic cameras only

Exploratory or complex camera paths cause the AI to fabricate detail — simple moves stay truest.

−
Unpredictable results

Each generation is unique — expect iteration before arriving at the ideal clip.

−
Short sequences only

Not a replacement for full animation. Works best as short, punchy supporting content.

Lessons Learned

In practice

"Quality comes down to the strength of the initial prompt and knowing when to restart rather than over-iterate."

01
Prompt quality is everything

Specific, directional language — camera type, movement style, pace — delivers far more consistent clips than vague direction.

02
Know when to restart

If the output isn't working, starting fresh usually beats trying to salvage a bad generation.

03
Edit clips together

Trimming, reversing, or speeding up several clips creates surprisingly polished narratives in very little time.

04
They fill an important gap

Dynamic stills won't replace traditional animation, but they make movement-driven content accessible without the production overhead.
